# Product Requirements Document: ZYNTH Digital Agency Website

## 1. Introduction
### 1.1 Purpose
This Product Requirements Document (PRD) outlines the vision, scope, and functional requirements for the ZYNTH digital agency website. The goal is to create an ultra high-end, award-winning online presence that showcases ZYNTH's expertise in web development, digital marketing, and graphic design through a unique, immersive, and visually stunning black-and-white aesthetic with advanced 3D animations and futuristic typography.

### 1.2 Scope
The ZYNTH website will serve as a primary touchpoint for potential clients, demonstrating the agency's capabilities and design philosophy. It will feature interactive 3D elements, premium momentum-based scrolling, and a distinctive visual style to establish ZYNTH as an absolute leader in the high-end digital solutions market.

### 1.3 Target Audience
Ultra high-net-worth individuals, enterprise decision-makers, and luxury brands seeking bespoke, cutting-edge digital solutions.

## 4. Core Experiences & Page Specifications

### 4.1 The Monolith (Home / Hero)
* **Visual:** A massive, monolithic 3D rendering of the word "ZYNTH" suspended in a pure black void.
* **Interaction:** The text distorts, refracting like liquid metal or dark glass based on cursor movement and device gyroscope. 
* **Transition:** The first scroll shatter-morphs the typography into abstract particles, pulling the camera down the Z-axis into the agency's realm.

### 4.2 The Arsenal (Services)
Represented as three distinct topological zones in a continuous WebGL canvas:
1.  **Web Development:** Visualized by a complex, constantly assembling 3D wireframe and brutalist architecture.
2.  **Graphic Designing:** Visualized by morphing, high-tension cloth simulations and typography bending around invisible spheres.
3.  **Digital Marketing:** Visualized by aggressive, rapid-fire kinetic data streams and geometric nodes connecting in real-time.

### 4.3 The Proof (Portfolio)
* **Interaction:** A 3D spatial carousel. Projects are presented as floating, monolithic slabs of monochrome material.
* **Hover Effect:** Hovering over a slab triggers depth-map displacement shaders, making flat 2D images pull apart into 3D layers.

### 4.4 The Tactile Form (Contact)
The contact experience must transcend standard HTML. It will simulate a highly satisfying, physical interaction while strictly maintaining the futuristic B&W aesthetic.
* **The Setup:** The camera pans down to an ultra-realistic 3D rendering of high-gsm textured matte paper on a sleek, light-absorbing black desk.
* **The "Marker" Interaction:** When the user types, a 3D fluid simulation renders thick, glossy black ink bleeding slightly into the paper fibers in real-time. It mimics a bold, premium marker.
* **The "Eraser" Interaction:** Standard backspacing is replaced by a physical mechanic. Deleting text summons a sleek, brutalist geometric block (the "eraser") that physically sweeps over the text. It uses a custom dispersion shader to smear and lift the ink off the paper organically.
* **The Submission:** Clicking submit physically folds the paper into a sharp geometric shape (e.g., an origami-like plane or dart) which flies off-screen, followed by a haptic ripple effect.

## 5. Design System & Aesthetic
### 5.1 The "Anti-AI" Mandate
Zero generic generative aesthetics. No Midjourney-style glossy renders. All visual complexity must be derived from raw math, custom 3D modeling, procedural noise, and bespoke WebGL shaders. 

### 5.2 Color & Light
* **Primary Palette:** Pure Black (`#000000`) and Pure White (`#FFFFFF`).
* **Depth:** All greys and gradients are generated entirely by real-time 3D lighting, shadows, and raymarching, rather than flat CSS colors.

### 5.3 Typography
* **Selection:** Big, bold, premium licensed fonts only (e.g., Monument Extended, Neue Montreal, bespoke variable fonts). 
* **Behavior:** Typography acts as 3D geometry. It is kinetic, reacting to scroll momentum and spatial camera panning.

## 6. Technical Stack & Architecture

| Component | Technology / Framework | Purpose & Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Frontend Shell** | Next.js (React) | Robust routing, SEO, and lightning-fast initial payload delivery. |
| **3D Engine** | Three.js + React Three Fiber | Core rendering engine for all 3D geometry and environments. |
| **Shaders** | Custom GLSL | Liquid metal, glass refraction, physical ink bleeding, and eraser dispersion effects. |
| **Animations** | GSAP + ScrollTrigger | Master timeline control for syncing 3D camera moves with the scrollbar. |
| **Scrolling** | Lenis (Virtual Scroll) | Physics-based, momentum-driven scrolling to eliminate browser jitter. |
| **Asset Pipeline** | DRACO & KTX2 / Basis | Ultra-compression for 3D models and textures to ensure zero-latency loading. |
| **Backend / CMS** | Sanity.io + Node.js | Headless content management and secure form data processing. |
| **Hosting** | Vercel / AWS Edge | Global CDN delivery to ensure sub-second global load times. |

## 7. Non-Functional Requirements
### 7.1 Performance & Quality Assurance
* **Frame Rate:** A locked 60 FPS (120 FPS on ProMotion displays) is mandatory. Frame drops during WebGL transitions are considered critical bugs.
* **Load Time:** Initial payload < 1.5MB. Time to Interactive (TTI) < 1.8 seconds.
* **Hardware Scaling:** Dynamic GPU detection. High-end devices receive real-time raymarching; lower-end mobile devices receive baked lighting to preserve the 60fps target.

### 7.2 Security
* Strict SSL/TLS encryption.
* Sanitization of all inputs on the 3D contact form to prevent injection attacks (OWASP Top 10 compliance).

### 7.3 Accessibility
* A visually hidden, perfectly structured HTML DOM must exist beneath the WebGL canvas to ensure flawless screen-reader compatibility (WCAG 2.1 AA) and perfect technical SEO, despite the heavy visual complexity.

* **Spatial Computing Integration:** Ensuring the Three.js environment is architected so it can be seamlessly ported to WebXR for Apple Vision Pro and Meta Quest experiences.
* **Multi-language Support:** Dynamic font loading strategies to handle kinetic 3D typography in multiple character sets (e.g., Japanese, Arabic) without breaking the WebGL geometry.
